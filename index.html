About

I am a first year PhD student in Systems at Cornell University. My advisor is Samitha Samaranayake. My research interests lie in the intersection of transportation systems engineering and theoretical computer science. I am interested in developing (or simply using) state-of-the-art algorithms for transportation-related applications. On the theory side, I am interested in online algorithms, approximation algorithms, and parametrized algorithms. On the applications side, I am interested in incorporating a demand-responsive component in public transportation. In particular, I am interested in i) the integration of public transportation and mobility on-demand, and ii) the operations of bus and shuttle systems with pop-up stops.

I received a Bachelor of Science in Civil Engineering and a Minor in Computer Science from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. While at Illinois, I worked with Dan Work (now at Vanderbilt University). My research included traffic esimation in safety-critical environments and machine-learning in freight-rail applications. Between Illinois and Cornell, I was a Bosch Energy Research Network (BERN) intern at Bosch North America. My supervisor was Shyam Jade, PhD. My work included city-scale agent-based traffic simulation as well as applied machine-learning.
