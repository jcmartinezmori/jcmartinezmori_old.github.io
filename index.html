About

I am a second year PhD student in Systems at Cornell University. My advisor is Samitha Samaranayake. My research interests lie in the intersection of transportation engineering, urban planning, operations research, and computer science. In particular, I am interested in improving our transportation systems with novel operational models and algorithms that emphasize i) a principled approach to problem solving and ii) real-world practical considerations. On the practical side, I am interested in the broader impacts of our transportation systems, especially as it pertains to the inception of high-occupancy modes of transportation and the reduction of single-passenger trips. On the theoretical side, I am broadly interested algorithms, including online algorithms, approximation algorithms, and parametrized algorithms. I am fond of algorithms that exploit the structure of particular kinds of problem instances, making them 'easier' than they would otherwise.

I received a Bachelor of Science in Civil Engineering and a Minor in Computer Science from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in May 2017. At Illinois, I was advised by Dan Work (now at Vanderbilt University). My research included traffic esimation in safety-critical environments as well as applied machine-learning for freight-rail systems. Between Illinois and Cornell, I was a Bosch Energy Research Network (BERN) intern at Bosch North America. My supervisor was Shyam Jade. My work included city-scale agent-based traffic simulation as well as applied machine-learning for i) population synthesis, and ii) studying engine data.
