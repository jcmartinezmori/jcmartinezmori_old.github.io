About

I am a PhD student in the Center for Applied Mathematics at Cornell University, where I am fortunate to work with Samitha Samaranayake. My research interests are in the fields of combinatorial optimization and online decision-making.

My career path took a dramatic turn after my first semester at Cornell, when I took courses with Bobby Kleinberg and David Shmoys. Through their courses I discovered my passion for mathematics and theoretical computer science, which led me to rethink my doctoral plan and ultimately transfer between academic programs. David and Bobby were both very supportive in this transition and are now in my dissertation committee.

I received a Bachelor of Science in Civil Engineering and a Minor in Computer Science from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in May 2017. While at Illinois, I was advised by Dan Work (now at Vanderbilt University). My research included traffic esimation in safety-critical environments as well as applied machine-learning for freight-rail systems. Between Illinois and Cornell, I was a Bosch Energy Research Network (BERN) intern at Bosch North America. My supervisor was Shyam Jade. My work included city-scale agent-based traffic simulation.
